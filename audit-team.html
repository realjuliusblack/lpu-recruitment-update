What the Audit Team actually does.

Department-watches-itself. Four passes every run. Four flavors of output.

Scheduler heartbeats: every cron job the system runs (LotusOne ingest, LocumSmart ingest, Qualification hold reminders, LEIE monthly refresh, and more) has an expected cadence. The Audit Team reads the last-run record for each and raises coverage drift the moment a job stops firing.

Invariants: nine system-wide rules that should always be true. Things like no stuck-sourced jobs older than a day, vendor auth tokens fresh, no orphaned approval requests, published-trend not collapsing. Each runs every audit cycle.

Reconciliations: every headline number that appears in a daily digest gets cross-checked against its source of truth. Jobs on the board reconciled against Supabase, apply-form submissions against GHL, background gates against the tool log. A contradiction means at least two things disagree, and that raises a different color than either a green or a red.

Self-test: the audit itself gets audited. Coverage drift checks whether every registered subsystem actually got a score this run. If a subsystem silently fell off the schedule, the Audit Team catches its own blind spot.
