Think of it like an auditor.

The analogy
A good auditor shows up on a schedule. Checks the books. Cross-references every number. Tells you what doesn't add up before it becomes a problem.

Most days it reports everything green. The point is that on the day it isn't, you know right away, not a week later.

What it checks.

Four passes, every run.

Pass 1
Did every job run?
Flags anything that stopped firing.
Pass 2
Are the rules still true?
Nine invariants checked every run. No stuck jobs. No stale logins.
Pass 3
Do the numbers match?
Board says 95, database says 97? Flagged as a contradiction.
Pass 4
Is the audit working?
Catches its own blind spots. A missing check is itself a finding.
