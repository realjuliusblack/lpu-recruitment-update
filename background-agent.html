What the Background Check Agent actually does.

Parallel, non-blocking, never auto-disqualifying. Software flags the issues. Humans decide.

When a physician clears Qualification: the Background Check Agent runs in parallel. It queries CMS NPPES to confirm the NPI is real and active, checks the HHS OIG exclusion list (monthly refreshed) for any match by NPI or name, and inspects both reference form responses the doctor submitted earlier in the flow.

The negative-signal rule is explicit: any reference with work-again of No, or any of the four 1-to-5 ratings at 3 or below, counts as a flag. Missing NPPES record, any exclusion match, or stale reference data also flag. Clean checks pass silently and the physician keeps moving through the rest of the pipeline.

When something flags: the physician is not rejected. Their main status never changes. The flags accumulate under a separate background state, and Tyson sees the case in Slack with Approve-Clear and Confirm-DQ buttons. Only Tyson's click can set a terminal rejection.

What we don't do: no Checkr, no paid background vendor. No NPDB for the MVP — hospitals handle that at credentialing. We're the pre-screen, not the full occupational risk suite.
