Think of it like a watchdog.

The analogy
A good watchdog stays quiet most of the time. Only barks when something is actually off. Never bites on its own.

That is this agent's whole job. It checks every qualified doctor against the sources that matter. Most come back clean and nobody ever has to look. The rare ones that flag, Tyson sees with a short summary and two buttons: clear them or confirm a decline.

It runs in parallel with the rest of the pipeline. Nothing waits on it. The main flow keeps moving.

What it does for every qualified doctor.

Four checks. All running at the same time. Usually finished before the next step even starts.

Check 1
Identity is real.
Looks up the doctor's NPI in the federal registry. Confirms name, specialty, and active status match what they told us.
Check 2
Not on the exclusion list.
Checks the federal healthcare exclusion list. If any doctor is on it for past issues, we need to know before a facility ever sees them.
Check 3
References sent and read.
Emails both clinical references the doctor submitted. When replies come in, the agent reads them and flags anything concerning.
Check 4
Tyson gets the flags.
Anything off the agent saw goes to Tyson. Clear it or confirm a decline. One click either way.

Three rules that keep it safe.

This is the part that has to be done right. Here's how.

1
Never auto-disqualifies.
The agent cannot reject a doctor by itself. Every flag waits for Tyson. No matter what the sources say.
2
Runs in parallel, never blocks.
The main pipeline does not wait for the background check. If references are slow, presentation can still move forward. The two flows stay independent.
3
Clear scope. No scope creep.
Identity, exclusion list, and clinical references. That's it. We are the pre-screen. The hospital runs the full credentialing file at hire.

What's next.

Certified and running. A few polish items.

Next
Slack console
A flag-and-resolve panel where Tyson gets a short summary with two buttons. All the logging is already in place. The Slack surface lights up the same week we provision the workspace.
Ongoing
Federal list refresh
The federal exclusion list updates on the first of every month. A daily check raises an alert if our copy is older than 35 days. Fail-closed means we never check against stale data.
Later
Skip when the client already checks.
Some hospitals run their own full background check every time, especially for short-notice fills. For those clients, ours is duplicate work. A small switch will let Tyson skip it per client when they ask.

The bottom line.

Every doctor gets checked. Most pass quietly. The rare flag goes to a human with one click either way. The machine never decides alone.

Fast where speed matters. Human where safety does.
