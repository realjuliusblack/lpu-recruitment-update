Two Ways In

Portal or Slack. Same agent either way.

Sometimes the portal is the right place. Sometimes you are already in Slack with the team. Top Bot lives in both. Approvals work in both. Status checks work in both. You choose the surface that fits the moment.

In the Portal
Deep work
Browse the full job tracker. Configure policy. Read the audit log. Long-form chat with the agent. The full command center experience.
In Slack
In the flow
Ask a quick question, get an answer. Approve an item with one tap. Get notified when something needs attention. No context switch.
Who sees what
Slack knows who you are. Ali gets full access. Ahmed gets full access. Tyson gets his own queue and the tactical side. Permissions match the portal. Same agent, same rules, either surface.
Policy Dial

You choose what's automatic. Change it any time.

Every action the system can take has a dial. Set it to automatic, to needs-your-approval, or off entirely. Tighten when you want more control. Loosen when you trust it. No developer in the loop.

Automatic
Runs itself
Routine moves the system is trusted to make on its own. Reads, internal updates, dashboard refreshes.
Needs Approval
Checked first
Drops into the approval queue. Nothing happens until a human says yes. Default for anything client-facing.
Hard Block
Not allowed
The system will refuse to do this, period. Use it when an action is off the table for now.
Example starting point
Internal writes on automatic. External outreach needs-approval. High-risk categories hard-block until you say so. Every setting is yours to move.
Self-Healing

It catches itself. Before anyone else has to.

Things go wrong. A source goes quiet. A data field comes back malformed. A process takes too long. The system notices, reacts, and keeps working on what it still can. Only pulls you in when it genuinely needs you.

Catches its own errors
Tries the fallback
When the first approach fails, it knows the second. When the second fails, it knows to stop and log rather than guess.
Logs every incident
Nothing disappears
Every failure is written down with full context. You can always look back and see exactly what happened and when.
Escalates to the right person
Only what matters
If the system cannot recover, it pings the right person in Slack with a one-line summary and a link to the full detail.
Keeps the rest running
Partial beats none
A problem in one corner does not stop the whole pipeline. Everything else that can run, runs.
