Think of it like a scout.

The analogy
A scout goes out. Finds what's new. Describes it plainly. Brings it back. Does it again in a few hours.

That is this agent's whole job. It checks the places where physician jobs show up. Writes each one in your voice. Posts it to your board. Then it goes out again.

Before this, someone had to click through vendor portals, copy-paste roles into a spreadsheet, and rewrite each description. That job disappeared. The scout never takes a break.

What it does every few hours.

Four simple steps. Done over and over. No one has to push a button.

Step 1
Check every source.
Looks at the vendor portals that list locum roles. Pulls down anything new since the last check.
Step 2
Skip duplicates.
The same hospital sometimes lists a role in two places. The agent recognizes it and keeps one clean version.
Step 3
Write the description.
A short, plain-English description in your voice. No corporate fluff. No recruiter-speak.
Step 4
Post it.
The role lands on your public job board within minutes. Doctors and candidates see it right away.

Three rules that keep it safe.

The scout is fast. These rules make sure fast does not turn into sloppy.

1
Never invents information.
If a vendor does not provide a rate or a start date, the agent leaves that field blank. It never guesses to make a post look complete.
2
Dedups before it posts.
Same role, different source? The agent catches it and keeps one version. The board stays clean.
3
Emails hospitals need a human yes.
When a hospital emails a role directly, the agent drafts and waits for Ali's one-click approval before it posts. Direct-from-client is special.

The bottom line.

The scout works around the clock. Your job board stays fresh. Nobody has to copy-paste a role ever again.

That's it. No secret. Just a small worker doing one job well, every few hours, every day.
