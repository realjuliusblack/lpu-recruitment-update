The Basics

What is a Managed Agent?

A tiny AI worker. One job. Strict rules. A human to escalate to. Five of them run the recruitment pipeline.

Like hiring a new employee.

The analogy
You don't write a rulebook for every situation. You hire someone smart, train them, and trust them to figure it out.

That's a Managed Agent. One job. The right tools. Clear rules. It works around the clock and pings a human when something is genuinely new.

What it does.

Four steps, done thousands of times a day.

Step 1
Something happens.
A doctor applies. A job posts. A client replies.
Step 2
It thinks.
Reads the situation. Decides the next move.
Step 3
It picks a tool.
Send. Score. Update. Ask. Every tool does one thing.
Step 4
It reads the result.
Worked? Move on. Stuck? Ping a human.

Why we need them.

Recruitment has a thousand little judgment calls.

The old way

Hard-coded rules for every case.

A programmer writes an if-then rule for every situation. New situation? It breaks, or a human picks up the slack.

Our way

An agent that thinks on its feet.

Rules where they matter. Judgment where nothing is obvious. Your team only sees the hard cases.

The five agents.

Each one owns one part of the pipeline.

Sourcing
Finds every open role. Posts to the board.
Physician Finder
Matches qualified doctors to new jobs. Reaches out.
Qualification
Scores every application in seconds. Flags reds for Tyson.
Background
Identity, exclusion, references. Never auto-DQs.
Presentation
Name clear out. Packet on yes. Follow-ups on silence.

Three promises.

Smart workers still need guardrails.

1
Never invent new tools.
They can only use the tools we gave them. No reaching into the rest of the business.
2
Hard rules live in the tools.
"Never skip Tyson" and "never auto-DQ" are baked in. The agent can't override them.
3
Everything is logged.
Every decision recorded. Replay any day, any case, any call.

And the team that watches the team.

An Audit Team runs twice a day. It checks every agent and cross-checks every number.

Most companies skip this layer. It's why you can trust the rest.
