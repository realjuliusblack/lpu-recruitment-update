The Basics

What is a Managed Agent?

Short answer: a tiny AI worker that does one job for us all day. It has rules. It has tools. It knows when to ask a human. Five of them run the whole recruitment pipeline.

Think of it like hiring a new employee.

The analogy
You don't write a rulebook for every possible situation. You hire someone smart, give them training, and trust them to figure it out.

That's what a Managed Agent is. We write one job description. We give it the tools it needs. We teach it what to escalate and what to decide. Then it works around the clock, follows the rules, and pings a human when something is genuinely new.

Old computer systems could only do exactly what they were told. If a situation wasn't in the rulebook, they broke. A Managed Agent thinks through a new situation the way a real employee would.

What does it actually do?

Here is a real day in the life of a single agent. Four simple steps, done thousands of times.

Step 1
Something happens.
A new physician applies. A job gets published. A facility replies to an email. The agent notices the event.
Step 2
It thinks.
It looks at what happened, checks what it already knows, and decides what the right next step is. Like any thoughtful worker.
Step 3
It picks a tool.
Send an email. Score the candidate. Update the record. Ask a partner system. Every tool does one job.
Step 4
It reads the result.
Worked? Move on. Looked wrong? Try a different approach. Genuinely stuck? Ping a human with the full picture.

Why we need them.

Recruitment has a thousand little judgment calls. Here's the difference.

The old way

Hard-coded rules for every case.

A programmer writes an if-this-then-that rule for every possible situation. New situation? Something breaks, or the work quietly stops, or a human has to pick up the slack.

It does not scale. It cannot read tone. It cannot decide whether a reply is a yes or a maybe. Anything unusual falls to the team.

Our way

An agent that thinks on its feet.

We give it the rules we actually care about. We give it the tools it needs. We let it handle the judgment. When something is new, it thinks it through.

Result: your team only sees the genuinely hard cases. Everything routine handles itself. The calendar stops getting eaten by busy work.

Three promises that make this safe.

Smart workers still need guardrails. Here are ours.

1
Agents never invent new tools.
They can only use the tools we gave them. They cannot reach into parts of the business they were not hired to touch.
2
Hard rules live in the tools, not in the agent's head.
Things like "never skip Tyson's review" and "never auto-disqualify a physician" are baked into the tools. The agent cannot override them even if it tried.
3
Everything is logged and reviewable.
Every decision an agent makes is recorded. We can replay any day, any case, any choice. If something goes wrong, we see exactly why.

And the team that watches the team.

On top of the five agents, we run an Audit Team twice a day. Its only job is to check the other agents and every system they touch.

It counts everything. It cross-checks the numbers against every source. If the job board says 95 jobs and the database says 97, the Audit Team raises a flag before the day gets going. The Manager and the client surface (Top Bot) both read the audit before they act.

This is the layer most companies skip. It is why you can trust what the other agents report.
