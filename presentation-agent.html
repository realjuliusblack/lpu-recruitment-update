Think of it like a handshake.

The analogy
A good handshake is short, clean, and clear. Says who you are, why you're here, what you're asking for. Nothing extra.

Yes means the full packet goes out. No means we move on. Silence gets one polite nudge. No spam, no pressure, no phone tag.

What it does.

Four moves, on repeat, for every match.

Step 1
Send the name clear.
Short email: name, specialty, NPI, availability. Yes or no?
Step 2
Read the reply.
Yes, no, ambiguous, or scheduling. Routes to the right next step.
Step 3
Packet on a yes.
CV, references, rate. Everything the client needs.
Step 4
Follow up on silence.
48 hours, then 96. Then Tyson gets an alert.

Three rules.

Client-facing emails are high stakes.

1
Never books a call.
Every email resolves with a reply or a click. No Zoom links. No phone tag.
2
Safety net on every send.
Every client email routes through Julius first during rollout.
3
No dead ends.
No for this job is not no from LPU. Decline copy keeps the door open.

What's between here and real facilities.

One meeting. One flip.

Next
Tyson session
60 minutes to define full packet content, tone, and attachments.
Same week
Hold flip
Walk Tyson through approvals. Flip recipient from Julius to real client.
Then
Lights on
Real clients see name clears. VMS portal routes come later.

The bottom line.

Every qualified doctor reaches a client. Every reply handled. Every silence gets one polite nudge, not five.
