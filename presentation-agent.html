Think of it like a handshake.

The analogy
A good handshake is short, clean, and clear. It says who you are, why you're here, and what you're asking for. Nothing extra.

That is this agent's whole job. A two-line email to the client contact. Here is the doctor, here is their specialty, are they a fit for this job, yes or no. That's the handshake.

When the client says yes, the full packet goes out. When they say no, we move on. When they go quiet, a short polite nudge. No spam. No pressure. No phone tag.

What it does for every qualified doctor.

Four simple moves. On repeat, for every match.

Step 1
Send the name clear.
A short email to the client contact. Doctor's name, specialty, NPI, availability. Yes or no. No CV yet, no rate yet.
Step 2
Read the reply.
The agent reads the client's response. Yes, no, ambiguous, or scheduling question. Routes each one to the right next step.
Step 3
Send the full packet on a yes.
Clear yes means the full packet goes out right away. CV, references, rate. Everything the client needs to move to credentialing.
Step 4
Follow up on silence.
Nothing back? A polite nudge at 48 hours. Another at 96. After that, Tyson gets a direct alert. No machine loops ever.

Three rules that keep it safe.

Client-facing emails are high stakes. Here's what we locked in.

1
Never books a call.
Every email gets resolved with a reply or a button click. No Zoom links, no scheduling games. The client decides on their own time.
2
Safety net on every send.
During rollout, every client email routes through Julius first. We tune tone with Tyson before a single real facility sees anything.
3
No dead ends.
A no for this job is not a no from LPU. We staff many sites. The agent's decline copy keeps the door open for the next opportunity.

What's between here and presenting to real facilities.

One meeting. One flip.

Next
Tyson session
60 minutes to define the full packet content. What goes in it on a yes. What tone. What attachments. The name clear step is already built; the packet step depends on this conversation.
Same week
Hold flip
Julius walks Tyson through the approval flow once, then flips the recipient from Julius's inbox to the real client contact. Every send is already logged and reversible.
Then
Lights on
Real clients start seeing name clears. Vendor portal routes (Aya, LocumSmart, Sound) come as a separate build. Tyson handles those manually in the meantime.

The bottom line.

Every qualified doctor reaches a client. Every client reply gets handled. Every silence gets one polite follow-up, not five.

Short, clean, human where it matters.
