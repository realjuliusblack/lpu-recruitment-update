Think of it like a doorman.

The analogy
A good doorman welcomes every guest. Points the right ones in. Sends the wrong ones somewhere better. Never slams the door.

That is this agent's whole job. Every physician who applies gets a real answer right away. The ones who look like a fit move through. The ones who do not get a tailored, human explanation that keeps them warm for the next opportunity.

Before this, applications piled up. Some doctors waited a week for a reply. Some never got one. That is over. Every doctor hears back the same day.

What it does when a doctor applies.

Four steps. Usually done in under two seconds.

Step 1
Read the application.
The agent picks up the submission the moment it lands. Reads license, certs, specialty, malpractice, and availability.
Step 2
Score it.
Green means looks-good. Yellow means one thing to clarify. Red means probably not a fit for this particular job.
Step 3
Send the right email.
The doctor gets a tailored reply immediately. No template blast. The tone matches where they landed on the score.
Step 4
Route to Tyson if needed.
Green flows through on its own. Red waits for Tyson to confirm before any DQ language goes out. Every reply Tyson approves with one click.

Green, yellow, red. No surprises.

The three outcomes. Each one has a clear next step.

Green

Looks good.

Strong fit. The doctor gets a warm hello with next steps. They move into the background check and presentation pipeline on their own.

Yellow

One thing to clarify.

Close to fit, but something needs a quick question. The doctor gets a specific, respectful ask. Tyson sees an FYI note in case it matters.

Red

Probably not this job.

Not a fit for this role. The doctor hears from us with a door-open note (LPU staffs many hospitals). Tyson confirms every red before it goes out. The machine never decides alone.

Three rules that keep it safe.

Speed is only good if it is also careful. Here's what we locked in.

1
The machine never rejects anyone.
Only Tyson confirms a red. The agent drafts the decline; Tyson clicks approve. No physician ever gets a rejection from software.
2
Every reply is human-checked.
When a doctor writes back, the agent drafts the answer. Tyson reviews it. One click and it ships. No canned replies, no ghosting.
3
The door stays open.
LPU staffs many hospital sites. Red for one job is not red for LPU. The copy reflects that. Every decline sets up a future conversation.
