Think of it like a fair-price clerk.

The analogy
A fair clerk reads the facility's offer. Checks what the market really pays. Takes a thin, transparent fee. Hands the rest to the physician.

Before, rates lived in one person's head. A doctor had to ask. A facility had to guess. Now the math runs on every job the board ingests, and everyone sees the outcome instantly.

What it does.

Four steps. Every job that lands on the board.

Step 1
Read the offer.
Grab the facility-side rate the moment a job lands. Nothing gets published without one.
Step 2
Run the formula.
Platform margin, physician pay. One rule, applied everywhere.
Step 3
Check the floor.
Every specialty has a researched pay floor. Below the floor, the job pauses for human review.
Step 4
Publish or hold.
Passes the floor, it goes live. Fails it, Tyson sees it before any physician does.

Two wedges.

Same formula. Three parties. Everyone comes out ahead of the traditional stack.

Physician

Paid above market.

Every specialty floor lands above the competitor median for that role. The platform refuses to publish a job that would pay less.

Facility

Billed below the stack.

Traditional agencies keep roughly a third of every dollar. Rate Logic keeps a fraction of that. The savings come from thin fees, not from squeezing the doctor.

Platform

Thin by design.

One transparent per-hour fee. Small enough that both wedges exist. Consistent enough that every booking follows the same rule.

How floors are set.

Calibrated from the real market. Not from a spreadsheet.

1
Live competitor boards.
Every major locum board scraped for current postings. Real numbers from real open roles, not stale survey data.
2
Physician-reported actuals.
What physicians say they got paid on Reddit, Student Doctor Network, Physician Side Gigs, Sermo. Ground truth underneath the boards.
3
Per-role tiers.
Physicians, CRNAs, and advanced-practice roles each get their own uplift. No one-size-fits-all rule forced onto different economics.

Self-tuning.

The engine learns. Every week. Never silently.

Monday morning
Traffic-light digest
Apply rate, decline reasons, time-to-fill, repeat-physician rate. Green, yellow, red per specialty. One glance shows what needs attention.
Same morning
Floor-drift proposal
A Bayesian posterior weighs observed quotes and physician-stated rates against the current floor. Drift past threshold surfaces a proposal.
Always
Human in the loop
Every proposal lands in front of a person before a rate changes. The engine suggests. A human decides. Nothing drifts silently.

Safeguards first.

Four gates between computed and shipped.

Gate 1
Master switch.
One flip gates every outbound message. Off means nothing goes live anywhere. On means everywhere at once.
Gate 2
Apply-time snapshot.
The rate a physician sees when they apply is the rate they get. Stored immutably. No drift between board and offer.
Gate 3
Append-only audit.
Every computation, every config edit, every flip is captured. Any day's state reconstructs on demand.
Gate 4
Margin privacy.
Platform fees and facility bills stay on the inside. The public surface only shows physician pay.

The bottom line.

Every job on the board is priced by math. Physicians come out ahead. Facilities come out ahead. The platform stays thin enough that both wedges exist.

No guesswork. No spreadsheets. No hidden margins. A transparent rule, applied to every role, every time.
